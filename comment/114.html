Net Zero Energy Homes, the Next Standard in Housing

Tags: 
Net Zero Energy Homes
Sustainable
Green
Greenspring Energy
Insulation
Tweet

Net Zero Energy Homes, commonly referred to as (NZEB’s), are the wave of the future; and now that wave has crashed on Maryland with the first Net-Zero home built in the Baltimore suburb of Cockeysville.

The home is constructed mainly from concrete, has 6 inches of closed-cell spray foam insulation in the roof (meaning all the cells in the foam are completely closed, tightly packed together, and crevices filled with gas for maximum insulation capacity), PEX tubing radiant heat flooring (heated water travels through tubes installed under the floor, evenly distributing warmth), a 5 kilowatt photovoltaic solar panel system, a solar thermal hot water heater with two storage tanks, AND a propane backup.

The home belongs to Dr. Thaw Poon, and was built with the goal of achieving Net Zero Energy use in mind. He didn’t know if he could get it, but $0 was what he was shooting for.

Construction on the 10,776 square foot home was begun in the spring of 2009, with the whole project completed and moved into by December of the following year. Then, less than 6 months later, Dr. Poon achieved his goal and received his first net $0 energy bill in May of 2011.

A lot of factors went in to designing this uber energy efficient home, from the concrete, to the windows, the pitch of the roof, appliances, and of course the energy producing solar electric and solar hot water systems.
Greenspring Energy, located in Timonium, MD was the provider and installer of the 5kw solar electric panels, and the solar hot water system. Sandi Formanek, the area sales manager for Greenspring in charge of that part of the project described it as a “labor of love, love and efficiency.”

The net zero phenomenon, has gained ground across the USA, and the rest of the world, with many agencies getting on board to promote the idea of a net zero home.

In Canada they developed the first net zero housing with the Eco Terra House in Eastman, Quebec. Also in Canada the Net-Zero Energy Home Coalition actively promotes net-zero energy home construction.

In 2009, the 71 story Pearl River Tower in China was opened and serves as headquarters for the Guangdong Company. It incorporates renewable energy generation, energy efficiency, along with solar and wind energy generation.
In 2006 the United Kingdom it was announced that by 2016 all new construction homes will be designed as zero energy buildings. To encourage this, an exemption from the Stamp Duty Land Tax will be implemented. Taking more aggressive action in Whales; they are planning to implement the building of net zero homes by next year in 2012.

“Going Green isn’t just a current fad,” says Sam Lichtenberg of Greenspring Energy, “it is the fullest realization of investment potential. When you look at the big picture: implementing energy producing and energy efficiency products, you are saving your wallet and the world all at once. I’m proud to work at Greenspring because I know I’m making a difference one project at a time.”
