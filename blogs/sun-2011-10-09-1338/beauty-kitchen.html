Beauty in the Kitchen

Submitted by KyleeP on Sun, 2011-10-09 13:38

Buying natural, organic products takes extra effort--after all, finding a chemical- and paraben-free shampoo certainly isn't as easy as going to your local Rite-Aid and grabbing a bottle off the shelf--and, in many instances, some extra cash. My favorite cleanser is Farmaestethics Fine Herbal Cleanser (it only has five ingredients!), but an 8-oz. glass jar comes at a pricey $34.50. Suddenly the five-dollar tube of Neutrogena wash on the drug store shelf doesn't seem so bad, except for that whole being laden with chemicals thing...

Lucky for those of us who are concerned about both the environment and our wallets, a cheap solution to our skin and hair woes sits on our kitchen shelves. That solution is, of course, baking soda. Baking soda, or sodium bicarbonate, is a chemical compound, so it is not a strictly natural product in the sense that you can't go outside and dig it out of the ground. However, when you compare it to the list of ingredients in some other products, baking soda is definitely the more eco-friendly option.

I remember being fifteen and using baking soda mixed with a little of water occasionally on my teeth; I heard it would whiten them so, being too young to yet have a job and any income, I thought this would be an adequate substitute for the Crest Whitestrips I couldn't then afford. Little did I know, baking soda has plenty of other personal-care uses.

Mixed with a little bit of water (not too much water though--you don't want it to be runny!) baking soda works as a great exfoliant for the face and body. Also, instead of investing in a clarifying shampoo in addition to your regular shampoo, you can just use baking soda: sprinkle some baking soda on your hand with a dollap of your normal shampoo and lather. The baking soda removes yucky product build-up.

These are some of the ways I've put baking soda to use other than in a mixing bowl, but I know there are many more! How have you used baking soda in your daily routine? What other household staples have you found that also work for skin and hair?
