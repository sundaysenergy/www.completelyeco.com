eco + Halloween, how can it work?

Submitted by Megan on Fri, 2011-10-14 21:45

It seems like it has been an unusual Fall this year in Baltimore, unusually hot for some days, and then -more recently- unusually wet and rainy. But, as we near the middle of October, we see the reality of the season reflected in the food available at the Farmers' Market. Broccoli, cauliflower, and especially squash-of all kinds have been slowly taking over our dinner plates, as the role of peaches, basil, and tomatoes fades until next year…

With October comes Halloween, and since we now have a two-year-old on our hands, we have begun to think about what sorts of approaches we might want to take to this day that seems to be so dedicated to lifestyle choices quite different from the ones we choose.

To give you an idea of our thought process, here are some excerpts from the discussions we've been having, along with some of the questions we asked ourselves:
Our son is certainly physically able now to walk around the block trick-or-treating if we choose to go that route. With his older cousins' assistance, he surely could even push doorbells and say trick-or treat. But does he need to?… He eats such healthy food the rest of the year, why should we change things for Halloween? And we go to such lengths to avoid the types of excess packaging that is unavoidable with Halloween candy… Are we somehow depriving our child if we decide to have him skip this ritual?… No doubt as he gets older and learns more about Halloween it will be harder to avoid the whole trick-or-treating thing. But for this year, maybe we can get away without inviting all those artificial flavors into our lives?… Furthermore, what do we do about making choices for what to hand out to children who come to our door? We live in the city and can expect a fair number of children to knock on our door that night. Do we just go out and purchase candy that we would otherwise never spend money on just because it is expected? Do we buy something healthier to hand out, risking the unfortunately realistic possibility that those treats will be discarded and go to waste simply because they are "too healthy"? What about handing out some sort of non-edible treat such as a packet of seeds to plant? Maybe we should just turn our lights off and hide from the trick-or-treaters that night so we can avoid the whole thing?… I don't want to be a scrooge and not contribute towards a happy holiday for children, but can't help feeling like it is all a little out of hand…

So… at the moment our decisions for this year include making a costume and treat bag for our son to use with materials we already have and can use again afterwards. We will take him trick-or-treating to a few houses on our street so he can visit with the neighbors he knows. At our house we will be giving mini fair-trade chocolates or organic fruit snacks. We will deal with next year and the years after that as they come.

And finally, some general ideas to consider for making your Halloween a little healthier for your family and the planet :

Make a costume from things you already have, or buy a second-hand costume at a consignment/thrift store...Consider handing out treats that reflect a more earth-conscious lifestyle, whether it be fair trade chocolate, organic fruit snacks, or non-edible treats such as crayons or seed packets...Use a re-usable bag for your child's treat bag...Freeze the bulk of the evening's candy haul and take out a piece or two as a treat now and then.

What will your family be doing to make your Halloween more "green"?
