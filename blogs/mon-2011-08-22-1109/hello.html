hello

Submitted by Megan on Mon, 2011-08-22 11:09

My name is Megan and I will be posting here from one Mom's perspective, on implementing - and living with - a variety of eco-minded choices.

I live in Baltimore, in the city, with my partner Jason, our almost 2-year-old son, and our two dogs. Jason and I have always lived a pretty "green" lifestyle, being conscious of how our purchasing and lifestyle decisions might affect the earth, but as we thought about bringing someone else into the world it became increasingly important to us to be sure we were modeling the kinds of values that we planned to teach our child.

For now, I have promised to give you a brief introduction and description of some of the things our family does in our pursuit of a more earth-friendly lifestyle. As a way of a partial introduction, the following is a list of some of the choices that we make which I plan to talk/write about in more detail in future posts.

We have been using cloth diapers (and cloth wipes) exclusively since our son was born. We visit the Waverly farmers' market almost every Saturday throughout the year, and we shop at the downtown farmers' market on occasion throughout the summer. We try to purchase any other food items locally and/or organically, shopping as much as possible at what is now the new Baltimore Food Co-op (and what used to be Mill Valley), as well as Mom's Organic Market, and Whole Foods. When food shopping we use re-usable shopping bags and reusable containers for small fruit items, as well as for bulk goods. I cook a lot, with real food from scratch as much as possible. We don't have a microwave. When our son began eating solid foods we mostly just eased him into things by giving him bits and pieces of fruits and vegetables off our plates, but we also made some fruit and vegetable purees for him instead of using commercial baby food. We try hard not to buy plastic things or things with excess packaging; when deciding between two similar items package waste is often the deciding factor. When we eat out, we bring a container with us for leftovers. We make our own cleaning products, laundry detergent, deodorant, lotion, bread, yoghurt, granola… We have both a worm compost bin and an electric composter in our basement. We subscribe to a service that helps us to receive virtually no junk mail. When we want to buy things that aren't food, if we decide we really need to buy another "thing", we try to buy locally and used from a consignment/thrift store first. We are extra careful about recycling and not contributing to landfills. All the lightbulbs we use are low energy.

There are some things we aren't doing yet that we want to. There are other things we haven't figured out yet. We have grown vegetables and herbs in the past, but haven't been growing much the past couple years. We try to freeze fruits and vegetables when they are in season, have dabbled in some pickling, but haven't yet done much canning.

My hope with these posts is to share what have been our practical experiences in doing things, provide some ideas for things people might want to try, and to offer different ways you might try implementing changes in your household. I understand that all these choices aren't for everyone, and some people don't want to, or can't do things the ways we do them. Hopefully we can give several suggestions for different ways of making choices for yourself and/or your family. But I'd also like to get ideas for other things we can do, so please feel free to ask questions, make suggestions, and chime in in anyway you feel contributes to the conversation!

thank you!
