eco + Halloween, how can it work?

It seems like it has been an unusual Fall this year in Baltimore, unusually hot for some days, and then -more recently- unusually wet and rainy. But, as we near the middle of October, we see the reality of the season reflected in the food available at the Farmers' Market. Broccoli, cauliflower, and especially squash-of all kinds have been slowly taking over our dinner plates, as the role of peaches, basil, and tomatoes fades until next year…

Beauty in the Kitchen

Buying natural, organic products takes extra effort--after all, finding a chemical- and paraben-free shampoo certainly isn't as easy as going to your local Rite-Aid and grabbing a bottle off the shelf--and, in many instances, some extra cash. My favorite cleanser is Farmaestethics Fine Herbal Cleanser (it only has five ingredients!), but an 8-oz. glass jar comes at a pricey $34.50. Suddenly the five-dollar tube of Neutrogena wash on the drug store shelf doesn't seem so bad, except for that whole being laden with chemicals thing...

My Grand Stand

Last November, a friend of mine, who had never met a bacon cheeseburger or slice of pepperoni pizza he didn’t like, bought me Jonathan Safran Foer’s Eating Animals. It was a just-for-the-hell-of-it gift, and one he soon regretted giving. Thanks to that book--and his thoughtfulness--I became a vegetarian, forever to be a disoriented wanderer through the meat-packed landscape of American cuisine.

My name is Megan and I will be posting here from one Mom's perspective, on implementing - and living with - a variety of eco-minded choices.

I live in Baltimore, in the city, with my partner Jason, our almost 2-year-old son, and our two dogs. Jason and I have always lived a pretty "green" lifestyle, being conscious of how our purchasing and lifestyle decisions might affect the earth, but as we thought about bringing someone else into the world it became increasingly important to us to be sure we were modeling the kinds of values that we planned to teach our child.
